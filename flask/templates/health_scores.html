Los Angeles Health Scores Analysis

In this project I combine Los Angeles County's Department of Public Health restaurant inspections dataset with Yelp's page listing. I aim to see if there are any correlations between the health score and quality of a restaurant in terms of reviews and price.

The Data

The Public Health Dataset has inspections for over 44,000 places that serve food in LA County - an area of 10 million people. The first challenge was to process this data. Yelp's API only allows 5,000 calls per day, and it takes two queries to get the full restaurant details. For the sake of time, I reduced the size of the dataset by focusing on independently owned local restaurants.

I filtered out ~25,000 markets (Von's, Whole Foods, Convenience Stores) and chains (KFC, Subway, Sizzler). Hopefully this makes the data more interesting and diverse. I assumed franchises would have stringent policies to ensure high scores to protect brand image (this assumption can be investigated at a later date).

The final dataset contains approxmitely 16,000 restaurants across all zip codes in LA county.

Health Scores and Grades

The health grade distribution shows that A grades are the majority, with B taking about 1/5th of the share, and C's being rare at 2%.

How Grading Works

A facility starts with a score of 100 points and for each code violation loses points.

For instance if a facility does not change the lining in a bread basket between customers, that is a MINOR infraction worth 2 points

If a facility serves ground beef with over 30% fat content that is a MAJOR infraction worth 4 points.

A score of

  • 90 to 100 receives Grade A
  • 80 to 89 receives Grade B
  • 70 to 79 receives Grade C
  • 0 to 69 does not get a letter, but a card displaying the score instead.

A restaurant scoring under 70 twice within 12 months are subject to closure, and any major infractions may require closure regardless of score.

Score Distribution

Instead of a single bell curve, the score distribution looks tri-modal with peaks occuring around 97, 85, and 76, each having a slight right-skew and an edge peak. Perhaps the multi-modality reflects that there are different types of retaurant owners, each with different sensitivies towards what they view is sanitary.

The edge peak boundaries between grades are curious. Specifically look at the large differences between 90-89 and 80-79. It seems like inspectors are using their discretion to push boarderline cases to the next highest grade.

Also values under 70 are rare, there is only one occurance (at 65). This is most likely due to the threat of closure for low scores leading restaurants to request a re-inspection if they did poorly. My data only includes the most recent inspection, but after looking through the complete dataset again there are only around 5 cases of below 70.

Do health scores correlate to higher yelp reviews?

There seems to be a slight positive correlation between health score and yelp review.

We saw from the grade distributions that there is not much data in the C range (2%), if we had more points the averages may show less variance and converge towards the regression line.

If we look at the edge peaks from the score distribution (79 and 89) we see the scores jump.

Looking at a normalized distribution of stars, it seems higher health scores have more 4 star restaurants and fewer 3 stars. All other scores are relatively evenly distributed. 4 stars are positvely correlated with health score (R=0.56) and 3 are negativly correlated (R=0.70). Again below 80 the data is very high in variance.
