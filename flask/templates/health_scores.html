Los Angeles Health Scores Analysis

In this project I combine Los Angeles County's Department of Public Health restaurant inspections dataset with Yelp's page listing. I aim to see if there are any correlations ...

The Public Health Dataset has ratings for over 44,000 places that serve food in LA County: an area of 10 million people. Yelp's API only allows 5,000 calls per day, and it takes two queries to get the full restaurant data. For the sake of time, I reduced the size of the dataset.

I filtered the dataset to focus on independently owned local restaurants. I removed large buisnesses like Supermarkets [Von's, Whole Foods, Smart & Final] and Restaurant Chains [KFC, Subway, Sizzler's]. The resulting set is about half the size at 19,000.

A breakdown of the Health Grade shows that A grades are the majority, with B taking about 1/5th of the share, and C grade restaurants being rare at 2%.

A facility starts with a score of 100 points, and for each code violation loses points.

For instance if a facility does not change the lining in a bread basket between customers, that is a MINOR infraction worth 2 points

If a facility serves ground beef with over 30% fat content, that is a MAJOR infraction worth 4 points.

A score of:

  • 90 to 100 receives an A
  • 80 to 89 receives an B
  • 70 to 79 receives an C
  • 0 to 69 does not get a letter, but a card displaying the Score instead.

A restaurant scoring under 70 twice within 12 months are subject to closure, and any major infractions may require closure regardless of score.

The score distribution looks tri-modal, with peaks occuring around 97, 86, and 76.

The boundaries between grades look abnormal. Specially look at the difference between 90-89 and 80-79. It seems like inspectors are using their discretion to push boarderline cases to the next highest grade.
